Crime in Berkeley, CA

Run by the FBI, the Uniform Crime Reporting (UCR) Program defines eight crimes as Part I offenses: criminal homicide, forcible rape, robbery, aggravated assault, burglary, larceny (excluding motor vehicle theft), motor vehicle theft, and arson.

Data from the City of Berkeley Open Data portal shows the annual totals of UCR Part I crimes, except for arson, reported to the Berkeley Police Department since 1985. The official description of the dataset can be found on the portal website. The crime definitions stated below are as defined by the UCR Program on the UCR program website.

Violent Crimes

Criminal Homicide

Murder and Nonnegligent Manslaughter: the willful (nonnegligent) killing of one human being by another.

Deaths caused by negligence, attempts to kill, assaults to kill, suicides, and accidental deaths are excluded. Justifiable homicides are classified separately.

Manslaughter by Negligence: the killing of another person through gross negligence.

Deaths of persons due to their own negligence, accidental deaths not resulting from gross negligence, and traffic fatalities are excluded.
